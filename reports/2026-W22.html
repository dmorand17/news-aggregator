Claude Opus 4.8 Released with Dynamic Workflows Anthropic

TeamPCP Is Poisoning Open Source Code at Unprecedented Scale Ars Technica

TeamPCP has corrupted hundreds of open source tools in near-weekly supply chain attacks — including breaching GitHub via a malicious VS Code extension that accessed roughly 3,800 internal repositories. The financially motivated group deploys ransomware post-breach, scrubs poisoned packages to cover tracks, and has turned what was an occasional threat into an ongoing systemic crisis for the global software supply chain.

Pope Leo XIV Issues First-Ever AI Encyclical: "Magnifica Humanitas" Simon Willison / WSJ

Signed 135 years to the day after Pope Leo XIII's landmark Rerum Novarum, "Magnificent Humanity" warns that AI risks reducing humans to "mere cogs" in an efficiency machine, draws a Tower of Babel parallel, targets the concentration of AI power among a few private actors, and calls for binding governance. Its accessible writing style has earned wide readership well beyond Catholic audiences.

Elon Musk Loses $150B OpenAI Lawsuit in Under Two Hours NBC News

Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers concurred. Musk announced an appeal, but the ruling ends the most high-profile AI legal battle of the decade.
