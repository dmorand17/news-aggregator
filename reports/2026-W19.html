Anthropic's CEO Says It Could Grow by 80 Times This Year New York Times

Anthropic's annual revenue run rate surpassed $30 billion last month, with CEO Dario Amodei projecting the company could grow 80× this year. The explosive growth has created acute compute constraints, prompting a cascade of landmark supply partnerships with SpaceX, Google, Amazon, Broadcom, Microsoft, and NVIDIA. At Code with Claude, Amodei framed the moment as AI crossing from research artifact to enterprise operating system — a bet that "intelligent" is becoming the new "digital."

SpaceXAI Signs Agreement with Anthropic for Massive Supercomputer Access Teslarati

SpaceX is providing Anthropic full access to the Colossus 1 data center in Memphis — over 300 megawatts of power and all 220,000 NVIDIA GPUs on-site. Announced at Code with Claude, the deal immediately allowed Anthropic to double Claude Code rate limits for Pro, Max, and Team plans and remove peak-hour caps. It follows a pattern of converting compute scarcity into negotiated supply agreements with the industry's largest infrastructure holders.

Claude Adds Self-Improving Agents Anthropic

Claude Managed Agents launched three major capabilities at Code with Claude: dreaming (analyzing past sessions to identify patterns and improve), outcomes (self-correcting based on predefined success criteria), and multiagent orchestration (delegating subtasks to specialized subagents). Harvey, Netflix, Spiral by Every, and Wisedocs are already using the system in production. The "dreaming" framing is Anthropic's public acknowledgment that its agents can now iteratively improve without human intervention between sessions.

Meta Plans Advanced 'Agentic' AI Assistant for Users Reuters

Meta is building a highly personalized AI assistant powered by its new Muse Spark model that can connect hardware and software tools, carry out everyday tasks, and learn from data with less human intervention than a chatbot. The company is targeting a launch before Q4 2026, putting it in direct competition with OpenAI's ChatGPT and Anthropic's Claude for consumer agentic workflows.

🧠 Frontier Models

6 items
GPT-5.5 Instant OpenAI

OpenAI promoted GPT-5.5 Instant to the default ChatGPT model on May 5, delivering 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims on high-stakes prompts in medicine, law, and finance, 30% more concise responses, and improved personalization that references past conversations and connected files. The 2× list price increase over GPT-5.4 is partially offset by the model generating fewer tokens per response — with real-world cost increases estimated at 49–92%.

Subquadratic Debuts a 12-Million-Token Context Window The New Stack

Miami startup Subquadratic claims to outperform GPT-5.5 on retrieval benchmarks with a 12-million-token context window (with a 50-million-token model promised soon), asserting its fully subquadratic architecture reduces attention compute by ~1,000× versus frontier models by making compute scale linearly with context length. However, researchers are demanding independent verification of the dramatic efficiency claims before accepting them at face value.

Apple to Let Users Choose Rival AI Models Across Its iOS 27 Features Bloomberg

Apple will allow users to select from multiple third-party AI providers — including Google and Anthropic — to power Siri, writing tools, and other Apple Intelligence features in iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 this fall. The shift marks Apple's strategic retreat from building best-in-class AI itself, positioning the platform as the preferred distribution layer for whatever model users trust most. For Google and Anthropic, direct integration into Apple's billion-device installed base is a massive new channel.

Code with Claude 2026: What Anthropic Actually Shipped Blake Crosley

Claude is now generally available across Microsoft Excel, PowerPoint, and Word (with Outlook in public beta), and ten ready-to-run finance agent templates for pitchbooks, KYC screening, and month-end close were released.
