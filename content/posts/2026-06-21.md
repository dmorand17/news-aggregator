## 🏛️ Policy & Regulation

**[When the Trump administration cracks down on Anthropic, who benefits?](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/21/when-the-trump-administration-cracks-down-on-anthropic-who-benefits/)** — *TechCrunch AI*

The Bureau of Industry and Security's June 12 directive barred Anthropic from distributing Fable 5 and Mythos 5 to any foreign national — including employees inside the US — after reports that a China-linked group had accessed the model and that Anthropic failed to act on a disclosed jailbreak. The order accelerated a conflict dating to February, when the Pentagon demanded Anthropic waive contractual restrictions on mass domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons. With both top models still dark, Gemini, Grok, and GPT-4o are emerging as the immediate beneficiaries of the enterprise vacuum.

## 🔒 Security & Safety

**[Agentjacking Attack Tricks AI Coding Agents Into Running Malicious Code](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/agentjacking-attack-tricks-ai-coding.html)** — *The Hacker News*

Researchers disclosed a new attack class that poisons Sentry error events with malicious commands indistinguishable from the tool's own remediation guidance — causing AI coding agents to execute attacker-controlled code on developer machines. The attack requires only a target's publicly embedded Sentry DSN (a write-only credential baked into websites and apps) to fire a rogue event at Sentry's ingest endpoint. Tested against Claude Code, Cursor, and Codex, researchers achieved an 85% success rate and found at least 2,388 organizations with exposed DSNs; stolen artifacts include environment variables, Git credentials, and private repository URLs. Sentry acknowledged the issue but declined to fix the root cause, patching only one specific payload string.
